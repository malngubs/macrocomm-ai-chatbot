1 WHAT IS A PROCEDURE AND WHY DO WE NEED IT?

1.1 Procedure Definition
A procedure is a detailed, step-by-step set of instructions that explains how to carry out
a specific process with an organisation.

It helps employees perform tasks consistently, correctly, and safely.
1.2 Reasons for a Procedure

e Standardisation - ensures that tasks are done the same way every time

e Clarity and guidance - provides clear instructions so employees know what to do

e Training tool - Helps onboard new staff quicker

e Compliance and safety - Ensures legal and safety requirements are met

e Accountability - clearly defies responsibilities, which in turn makes it easier to
track who did what and when.

1.3 Benefits of a Procedure

e Support for continuous improvement - All documented processes can be
reviewed and improved over time

e Efficiency and timesaving - reduce confusion

e Reduced errors - minimises mistakes and rework by providing clear guidance

e Risk management - lowers the chance of operational failures and errors

e Easier problem-solving - if an issue arises, this document helps identify at which
step things went wrong
